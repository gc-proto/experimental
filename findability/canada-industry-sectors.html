Industries and workplaces regulated by the federal government

Specific sectors, goods and services in Canada are subject to regulation by the federal government. Examples include:

  • food safety, labelling, import and export
  • drugs and medical devices
  • financial services
  • broadcasting
  • nuclear energy

To learn more about specific regulations, see:

  • Federally regulated industry sectors

The Canada Labour Code (the Code) defines the rights and responsibilities of workers and employers in federally regulated workplaces. The Code covers:

  • Rights and responsibilities of employers, trade unions and employees
  • Occupational health and safety
  • Standard hours, wages, vacations and holidays

Canada’s major industries

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) assigns a dollar value for goods and services produced and sold. In terms of GDP, the major sectors of the Canadian economy are:

  • Real estate and rental and leasing
  • Manufacturing
  • Mining, quarrying, and oil and gas extraction
  • Finance and insurance
  • Construction

When measured by numbers of people employed, the top sectors are:

  • Wholesale and retail trade
  • Health care and social assistance
  • Manufacturing
  • Professional, scientific and technical services
  • Educational services
  • Construction

How sectors and industries are defined

The classification system used by Statistics Canada to track Canada’s economic activity divides the economy into 20 sectors, of which four are largely goods-producing and sixteen are services-producing.

The system further divides these sectors into 99 subsectors, 323 industry groups, 695 industries and 922 Canadian industries.
