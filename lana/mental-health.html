Mental health help in Canada

Find mental health help in Canada. You can call, text, or chat with someone now, find services near you, and get support for your situation.

Get help now

If you or someone you know needs help right away, use one of the options below. You do not need to figure this out on your own.

Call 9-1-1

Call 9-1-1 now if:

  • you are in immediate danger
  • you need urgent medical care

Call or text 9-8-8: Suicide Crisis Helpline

Call or text 9-8-8 now if you or someone you know is thinking about suicide.

Suicide Crisis Helpline offers support that is:

  • available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
  • confidential
  • support in English and French
  • trauma-informed and culturally appropriate

You can call or text anytime, even if you are not sure what to say.

Talk to someone in your area

  • get help in your province or territory
  • call a crisis line for family or gender-based violence

Mental health and substance use

Mental health and substance use are closely connected. If you or someone you know needs help with substance use, support is available.

You can:

  • call a crisis or support line to talk to someone
  • find treatment services near you
  • access community programs and supports

Start with Get help with substance use to find services and support options.

More ways to get help

If you are not in crisis, you can still talk to someone.

You can contact a:

  • family physician
  • psychologist
  • mental health nurse
  • social worker

You can also talk to someone you trust, such as:

  • a counsellor
  • a spiritual leader

If you are not sure where to start, a family doctor or local health service can guide you.
