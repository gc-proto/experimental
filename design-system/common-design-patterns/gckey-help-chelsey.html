Help with GCKey

Use your GCKey username and password to sign in to most Government of Canada online accounts and services.

Anyone in any country can sign up and create a GCKey username and password to access online services.

GCKey is for signing in

A GCKey username and password can be used to sign in to any Government of Canada online account that supports GCKey.

It's not an account. Once you create a GCKey, you still have to register for each online account, so it can match your GCKey username and password to your account profile.

You usually only need one GCKey username and password to sign in to any government service that supports GCKey. For example:

  • create a GCKey username and password to register and reserve a campsite in My Parks Canada Reservation Account
  • then use your same GCKey username and password to register and sign in to My Service Canada Account to manage your Employment Insurance or Canada Pension

Note: GCKey usernames and passwords expire if they are not used for two years or more. If your GCKey expires you will need to sign up for a new one.

You can use the same GCKey with different computers. All internet browsers must have JavaScript and Cookies enabled for the GCKey to work.

How to create a GCKey username and password

  1. find the account or government service you want to register for
  2. then use the GCKey button to register if GCKey is supported for that account
  3. on the Welcome to GCKey page, click the Sign up button
  4. read and accept the Terms and Conditions of Use
  5. create your username
  6. create your password
  7. create your recovery questions
  8. enter a recovery email address (optional)

You can also create more than one GCKey.

Create your username

You can find rules for creating your username in the Username Checklist box on the Create your username page. You will not be able to proceed unless all requirements listed in that checklist are met.

As you enter a username a red “x” or a green checkmark will appear in the Username Checklist. The “x” means you have not met the username requirements. The checkmark means you have met the username requirements.

Each username must be unique. You will receive a message if the username you create has already been used.

Tips for creating a username

When creating your username, you should:

  • make it easy for you to remember but hard for others to guess
  • avoid using personal information, such as your name, Social Insurance Number (SIN), mailing address, or email address
  • keep your username secure and do not share it with anyone
Create your password

You can find rules for creating your password in the Password Checklist box. You will not be able to proceed unless all password requirements are met.

As you enter a password a red “x” or a green checkmark will appear in the Password Checklist. The “x” means you have not met the password requirements. The checkmark means you have met the password requirements.

Confirm your password

You must re-enter your password exactly as you entered it into the Create Your Password field. If both passwords do not match, you will not be able to proceed.

Tips for creating your password

When creating your password, you should:

  • make it easy for you to remember but hard for others to guess
  • avoid using personal information, such as your name, Social Insurance Number (SIN), mailing address, or email address
  • keep your password secure and do not share it with anyone
Create recovery questions, answers and hints

Recovery questions are used to help you if you forget your password.

Your questions, answers and hints should be easy for you to remember but hard for others to guess.

Recovery questions and answers are mandatory. To create yours follow these steps:

  1. for the first question, choose a recovery question from the Select a Recovery Question
  2. enter an answer to the question in the My Recovery Answer
  3. next, enter the name of someone you will remember in the Memorable Person
    • if you think you may forget the answer to this question, you can enter a hint in the Memorable Person Hint
  4. next, enter a date that you will remember in the My Memorable Date
    • if you think you may forget the answer to this question, you can enter a hint in the Memorable Date Hint
  5. select Continue
Add your email address in case you forget your username or password

On the Optional Account Recovery page you can add an email address. This email address will be used to help you recover your GCKey account if you forget your username or password.

You do not have to add an email address, this step is optional.

As you enter an email address a red “x” or a green checkmark will appear in the Email Checklist. The “x” means you have not met the email requirements. The checkmark means you have met the email requirements.

If your email address has already been used

If your email address is associated with another GCKey account, a warning message will appear indicating that your email address is already in use. Only one unique email address can be linked to an account. You will have the option to:

  • attempt to recover the username and/or password of the other GCKey account associated with that email address by following the process for username recovery or password reset
  • register a different email address
  • register the email address to your new GCKey account by completing the email address registration process. If you choose to complete this process, the system will then remove the email address from your other GCKey account
Creating more than one GCKey

You can have more than one GCKey. For example, you may want one for work vs one for personal accounts. If you do create more than one GCKey:

  • remember which username and password you use for each type of service
  • use a different recovery email address for each GCKey username and password pair

Note: If you have more than one GCKey, ensure you keep track of which GCKey you have associated with each government account. Once a GCKey is linked to a specific government account, you need to use the same GCKey username and password to sign in each time.

How to sign in using GCKey

When accessing a Government of Canada online account, ensure you sign in the same way each time. If you have previously used a GCKey to sign in, ensure you sign in with the same GCKey username and password you used to register for that online account.

How to sign in with GCKey:

  1. on the sign-in page for the account you want to use, select the GCKey button
  2. you'll see the Welcome to GCKey page
  3. enter the username and password you created when you first signed up for your GCKey
  4. click the Sign in button

Forget your username or password

If you've forgotten your GCKey username or password, you'll need to recover them through the account you want to use:

  1. find the account or government service you plan to use
  2. select the GCKey button to sign in to the account you want to use
  3. On the Welcome to GCKey page, select 
    • Forgot your username
    • Forgot your password
Forgot your username

If you selected Forgot your username

  1. enter your recovery email address:
    • you provided your recovery email either when you signed up for your GCKey username and password or at a later time
    • if you didn't add a recovery email address and have forgotten your username or password, you have to sign up for a new GCKey and register again with each of the accounts you use
  2. select Continue
  3. you will be brought to a screen that explains where you username and password will be sent. Select Continue
  4. check your email, you should receive an email from the Government of Canada with your GCKey username
  5. enter your username and password on the Welcome to GCKey page
Forgot your password

If you selected Forgot your password

  1. enter your GCKey username
  2. select Continue
  3. check your email, you should receive an email from the Government of Canada with a Confirmation Code
  4. enter the confirmation code you receive on the Forgot Your Password Step 2 of 4 page (this page should have automatically loaded for you after you entered your username and selected Continue on the Forget Your Password Step 1 page
  5. select Continue
  6. you will be prompted with a recovery question. This is a question you would have inputted the answer to when you first set up your GCKey account.
  7. enter your recovery answer in the My Recovery Answer field, select Continue
  8. enter a new password in the New Password field
  9. re-enter that same password in the Confirm New Password field
  10. select Continue
  11. select Continue again on the Forget Your Password Complete page

You can find rules for creating your password in the Password Checklist box. As you enter a password a red "x" or a green checkmark will appear in the Password Checklist. The "x" means you have not met the password requirements. The checkmark means you have met the password requirements. You will not be able to proceed unless all requirements are met.

Confirm your password

You must re-enter your password exactly as you entered it into the Create Your Password field. If both passwords do not match, you will not be able to proceed.

Tips for creating your new password:

When creating your password, you should:

  • make it easy for you to remember but hard for others to guess
  • avoid using personal information, such as your name, Social Insurance Number (SIN), mailing address, or email address
  • keep your password secure and do not share it with anyone

How to change information within your GCkey account 

First, sign in with GCKey:

  1. on the sign-in page for the account you want to use, select the GCKey button
  2. you'll see the Welcome to GCKey page
  3. enter the username and password you created when you first signed up for your GCKey
  4. click the Sign in button

You should see an Options menu on the right-hand side of the page. You can choose to:

  • Change your password
  • Change your recovery questions
  • Manage your email address

Change your password

If you select Change Your Password:

  1. enter your current password in the first field
  2. enter a new password in the Create Your New Password field
  3. enter that same password again in the Confirm Your New Password field
  4. select Continue

Change your recovery questions

If you select Change Your Recovery Questions:

  1. enter your current password in the first field 
  2. choose a recovery question from the Select a Recovery Question
  3. enter an answer to the question in the My Recovery Answer
  4. next, enter the name of someone you will remember in the Memorable Person
    • if you think you may forget the answer to this question, you can enter a hint in the Memorable Person Hint
  5. next, enter a date that you will remember in the My Memorable Date
    • if you think you may forget the answer to this question, you can enter a hint in the Memorable Date Hint
  6. select Continue

Manage your email address

If you choose Manage Your Email Address

  1. enter your current password in the first field
  2. review the Current Email Address field, it is the email address you currently have registered with GCKey
  3. enter the new email address you want to register in the New Email Address field
  4. re-enter that email address in the Confirm Your New Email Address field
  5. select Continue 

Note: If you do not want to have an email address associated with your GCKey account you can remove your email address:

  1. go to Manage Your Email Address
  2. choose Remove Email Address from the box on the right-hand side of the page

What to do if your GCkey has been revoked

If your GCKey has been revoked you cannot recover it. You must register for a new GCKey username and password by selecting the Sign Up button on the GCKey welcome page. Once your new account is created you can link it to an existing user profile by contacting the government department for which you are trying to access services.

If your GCKey has been revoked, it is most likely due to one of the following reasons:

  1. Your GCKey has expired. The system requires you to use your GCKey at least once every two years. Otherwise, your account will be revoked.
  2. Your GCKey username and password have been compromised. It is essential that none of your online accounts of any kind share the same username and password.

If you have any questions about why your GCKey has been revoked, please contact the government department for which you are trying to access services.

Revoke your GCKey

If you no longer want to use your GCKey, you can revoke it. This action cannot be undone.

To revoke your GCKey, first, sign in with GCKey:

  1. on the sign-in page for the account you want to use, select the GCKey button
  2. you'll see the Welcome to GCKey page
  3. enter the username and password you created when you first signed up for your GCKey
  4. on the Remove Email Address page enter your password in the Enter Your Current Password field
  5. select Continue
  6. click the Sign in button
  7. you should see an Options menu on the right-hand side of the page. From that menu choose Revoke Your GCKey.
  8. on the Revoke Your GCKey page enter your current password, then select Continue. 

Note: When you Revoke your GCKey it can no longer be used. All Government of Canada online services that you enroled with using this GCKey will no longer be accessible. To re-establish access to government online services you must sign up for a new GCKey and re-enrol with each of the services you wish to use.
