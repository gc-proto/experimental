Registering for a National Online Licensing System account

  1. Technical requirements

    To register and use the National Online Licensing System, you need:

    • Computer connected to the Internet
      • If you don't have it: Visit a public library or any other location that offers Internet services.
    • E-mail address
      • The Department will e-mail you when you have a message on the National Online Licensing System.
    • Printer
      • You need a printer to print your fishing licence, licence conditions, and payment receipts.
    • A Portable Document Format (PDF) Reader
      • A PDF reader will allow you to view and print your licensing documentation that is in your account in the National Online Licensing System.

  2. Choose how you want to register

    Your first choice is how you would like to sign in every time you use the National Online Licensing System

    Option 1: Use a GCKey username and password

    Or sign up for GCKey (if you don’t have it).

    Register with GCKey

    Option 2: Register through your online banking

    Register with a Sign-in partner

    You must have an account with one of our Sign-in Partners.

    Your banking ID and password are never shared with DFO and National Online Licensing System details are never shared with your financial institution.

    View list of Sign-In Partners

  3. In your account, create your profile

    If this is your first time using the National Online Licensing System, the account will take you through the steps of creating a profile with the Department to get a DFO passcode for the next time you sign in.

    Select self-registration

    After signing in to the system for the first time, you will be directed to a screen called “Registration Options.” Click on the “self-registration” link under the “Self-registration” section.

    Accept the terms and conditions

    The “DFO Registration – Terms and Conditions of Use” screen will appear. After reading the screen, click the “I accept” button.

    Enter your contact information

    The “DFO Registration – Basic details” screen will appear. Type in your first and last name, as well as your e-mail address and mailing address type. The other fields on this screen, such as your preferred language (e.g., English or French) are optional, so you may choose whether or not you wish to type in this information. When you have finished, click the “Next” button.

    Enter your postal code

    The “DFO Registration – Canadian Postal Code” screen will appear.Type in your postal code and click the “Next” button. This will automatically generate your mailing address on the next screen called, “DFO Registration – Canadian Civic Address.”

    Verify your address

    Verify that the address that appears on the “DFO Registration – Canadian Civic Address” screen is your address. If there is any missing information, you will be prompted to type in this information. If the address is correct, or when you have completed typing in the missing information, click the “Next” button.

    Enter your telephone number

    The “DFO Registration – North American Telephone Details” screen will appear. Type in your telephone number, including the area code. You may also choose to provide a fax number and/or a mobile number, but these are not required. When you are finished, click the “Next” button.

    Confirm profile details

    All of the information that you have provided will now appear on the “DFO Registration – Confirm profile details” screen. Take a few minutes to review this screen to ensure that the information is correct. If it is correct, click the “Save” button. If you need to change any of this information, click the “Back” button to return the screen on which you need to make any changes.

    Save your profile

    Once you click the “Save” button, the “National Online Licensing System – You are not enrolled” screen will appear. Since you do not have a DFO passcode, click your cursor in the box beside the “I do not have a National Online Licensing System passcode” message and then, click the “Register” button.

    It's not clear how I will receive the DFO passcode You will now be on the National Online Licensing System and may complete the tasks required of you.

Account help

Forgot your GCKey password or username

  • First select sign-in with GCkey
  • then select Forgot your username or Forgot your password

Use a different Sign-in partner

If you've moved to a new bank or credit union, and already sign in through Interac® Sign-in Partner, you may be able to switch:

  • first, select Interac® Sign-in Partner
  • then select Switch My Sign-in Partner from the menu bar

Lost your DFO passcode

Request a new DFO Passcode if you lost your Passcode, your Passcode expired, or you changed your GCKey username and password. If you make changes to your Sign-In Partner information you will need your Passcode reset.

To request a new DFO Passcode, contact Client Support.
