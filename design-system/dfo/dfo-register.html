Register for My Account for Individuals

  1. Gather your documents

    When you register for an account with the CRA, you will need:

    • Your assessed tax returns for the 2 most recent tax years
      • If you don't have them: Your tax return for at least one of the two most recent tax years must have been filed and assessed for you to register.
    • Your Social Insurance Number
      • Alternatives: a Temporary Tax Number (TTN) or Individual Tax Number (ITN)
    • Your date of birth
    • Your current postal code or ZIP code (if resident in Canada or the US)
    For the RAC registration page only

    Register with a Non-Resident Representative Number (NRRN)

    If you are a non-resident representative living in the United States, you can register for Represent a Client with a NRRN to access information and services on behalf of individuals and businesses.

    • Submit Form RC391, Application for a Canada Revenue Agency Non-Resident Representative Number (NRRN), and provide supporting documentation to prove your identity
    • Wait until you receive a letter in the mail with your NRRN
    • Register for Represent a Client with your non-resident representative number instead of a Social Insurance Number

  2. Choose how you want to register

    Your first choice is how you would like to sign in every time you use My Account

    Option 1: Create an ID and password

    Create a CRA user ID

    Create a new user ID and password to use exclusively with CRA My Account.

    Option 2: Register through your online banking

    Register with a Sign-in partner

    You must have an account with one of our Sign-in Partners.

    Your banking ID and password are never shared with the CRA and My Account details are never shared with your financial institution.

    View list of Sign-In Partners

    Option 3: Register through your province

    Register with a provincial ID

    Currently, only BC Services Card or MyAlberta Digital ID are supported.

    You must have an account with the province's website to register this way.

    View list of provincial partners

  3. Wait for your security code

    Once you complete the first step of your registration, a CRA security code will be sent to you by mail. The CRA security code has an expiry date, which will be indicated in the letter you receive.

    Before the expiry date, sign in with the option you registered with and follow the online instructions until you are prompted for the security code.

    Get a new security code

    If your security code expires, you will have to get a new security code issued to you.

    Need immediate access? Get limited access without a security code

    You can get limited service immediately, similar to a Guest account by beginning the registration process and proving your identity. You do not need to finish the full registration process (mail or call for a security code, set up security questions etc.) to access the following:

    • View status of your tax return
    • Notification preferences
    • Request a remittance voucher
    • View notice of assessment or reassessment (simple view, lines X, Y and Z)
    • View RRSP deduction limit and TFSA contribution room
    • Pay by pre-authorized debit (create new agreement)
